## What Is Local SEO?

Local SEO is the process of optimising your online presence to attract customers from relevant local searches. When someone searches "coffee shop near me" or "plumber in Manchester," local SEO determines who appears in those results.

If you have a physical location or serve customers in a specific area, local SEO is arguably more important than general SEO.

## The Local Search Ecosystem

Local results appear in three places:
1. **Google Maps Pack** — the map with 3 business listings at the top of results
2. **Organic local results** — regular blue links with local intent
3. **Google Maps app** — searches directly within Maps

Winning the Maps Pack is the highest-value goal for most local businesses.

## Step 1: Claim and Optimise Your Google Business Profile

Your Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) is the foundation of local SEO.

### Essential optimisations:
- **Complete every field** — name, address, phone, website, hours, category
- **Choose the right primary category** — be specific (e.g. "Italian Restaurant" not just "Restaurant")
- **Add secondary categories** — cover all your services
- **Upload high-quality photos** — exterior, interior, products, team
- **Write a compelling description** — include your main keywords naturally
- **Add your service area** — if you serve customers at their location

### Keep it active:
- Post updates weekly (offers, events, news)
- Respond to every review — positive and negative
- Answer questions in the Q&A section before customers ask them

## Step 2: NAP Consistency

NAP stands for Name, Address, Phone number. These must be **identical** everywhere online:

- Your website
- Google Business Profile
- Social media profiles
- Business directories
- Review sites

Even small inconsistencies (St. vs Street, Ltd vs Limited) can confuse search engines and hurt your rankings.

## Step 3: Build Local Citations

A citation is any online mention of your business's NAP. Build citations on:

- **Tier 1**: Google, Apple Maps, Bing Places, Facebook
- **Tier 2**: Yelp, TripAdvisor, Foursquare, Yellow Pages
- **Tier 3**: Industry-specific directories (e.g. Houzz for home services, Healthgrades for medical)

Use a tool like BrightLocal or Whitespark to find citation opportunities and track consistency.

## Step 4: Generate and Manage Reviews

Reviews are a major local ranking factor and directly influence whether customers choose you.

### Getting more reviews:
- Ask in person right after a positive experience
- Send a follow-up email with a direct link to your review page
- Add a "Leave us a review" link to your website and email signature
- Train your team to ask satisfied customers

### Responding to reviews:
- Thank positive reviewers by name and mention something specific
- Address negative reviews calmly and professionally — offer to resolve offline
- Never argue or get defensive in public responses

## Step 5: On-Page Local SEO

Your website needs to signal local relevance:

- **Include your city/region in title tags** — "Plumber in Bristol | ABC Plumbing"
- **Create location pages** — if you serve multiple areas, create a dedicated page for each
- **Embed a Google Map** on your contact page
- **Add LocalBusiness schema markup** — structured data that tells Google exactly who you are and where you operate
- **Get local backlinks** — from local newspapers, chambers of commerce, sponsorships

## Step 6: Track Your Local Rankings

Standard rank tracking tools don't show local results accurately. Use tools that track rankings by location:

- BrightLocal
- Whitespark
- Local Falcon (for Maps Pack tracking)

Monitor your Maps Pack position, organic local rankings, and Google Business Profile insights (views, clicks, calls).

## Common Local SEO Mistakes

- **Keyword stuffing your business name** — "Joe's Plumbing Best Plumber London" violates Google's guidelines
- **Using a virtual office address** — Google may suspend your listing
- **Ignoring reviews** — no response signals you don't care
- **Inconsistent hours** — update immediately when hours change

Local SEO rewards consistency and genuine engagement. A business that actively manages its online presence will always outrank one that set it up once and forgot about it.
