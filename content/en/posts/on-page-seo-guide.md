## What Is On-Page SEO?

On-page SEO refers to all the optimisations you make directly on a web page to improve its search rankings. Unlike off-page SEO (backlinks) or technical SEO (site infrastructure), on-page SEO is entirely within your control.

Get it right and you give every piece of content the best possible chance of ranking.

## 1. Title Tags

The title tag is the single most important on-page SEO element. It appears as the clickable headline in search results.

### Rules:
- **Length**: 50–60 characters (longer gets truncated)
- **Include your primary keyword** — ideally near the beginning
- **Make it compelling** — it's also an ad for your page
- **Unique for every page** — never duplicate title tags

### Examples:
```
❌ Home | My Website
✅ Keyword Research Guide: Find Keywords That Convert (2024)
```

## 2. Meta Descriptions

Meta descriptions don't directly affect rankings, but they heavily influence click-through rate — which does.

### Rules:
- **Length**: 150–160 characters
- **Include your primary keyword** — Google bolds it in results
- **Write a genuine value proposition** — what will the reader get?
- **Include a soft call to action** — "Learn how...", "Discover..."

## 3. Heading Structure

Headings (H1–H6) help both users and search engines understand your content hierarchy.

### Rules:
- **One H1 per page** — it's your page title, make it count
- **H2s for main sections**, H3s for subsections
- **Include keywords naturally** — don't force them
- **Make headings descriptive** — a user should understand the page structure from headings alone

## 4. URL Structure

Clean, descriptive URLs are better for both SEO and users.

```
❌ yoursite.com/p?id=1234
❌ yoursite.com/blog/2024/03/15/my-first-post-about-seo-and-how-it-works
✅ yoursite.com/blog/on-page-seo-guide
```

### Rules:
- Use hyphens, not underscores
- Keep it short and descriptive
- Include your primary keyword
- Use lowercase only

## 5. Content Quality and Depth

Google's Helpful Content system rewards content that genuinely helps users. Thin, generic content gets demoted.

### What makes content rank:
- **Comprehensiveness** — cover the topic thoroughly
- **Originality** — add your own insights, data, or perspective
- **Accuracy** — cite sources, keep information current
- **Readability** — short paragraphs, subheadings, bullet points
- **E-E-A-T** — demonstrate Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness

### Optimal content length:
There's no magic number. Write as much as the topic requires to fully satisfy user intent — no more, no less.

## 6. Keyword Optimisation

### Primary keyword placement:
- Title tag
- H1 heading
- First 100 words of the introduction
- At least one H2 subheading
- Meta description
- URL slug
- Image alt text (where relevant)

### Secondary keywords and LSI terms:
Sprinkle related terms naturally throughout. Don't stuff — write for humans first.

### Keyword density:
Aim for 1–2% density for your primary keyword. If it feels forced, it's too much.

## 7. Image Optimisation

Images affect both page speed (a ranking factor) and accessibility.

### Checklist:
- [ ] Descriptive file names (`seo-checklist.webp` not `IMG_1234.jpg`)
- [ ] Alt text on every image — describe what's in the image, include keyword where natural
- [ ] Compress images (use WebP format)
- [ ] Set explicit width and height attributes (prevents CLS)
- [ ] Lazy load below-the-fold images

## 8. Internal Linking

Internal links distribute page authority across your site and help users discover related content.

### Best practices:
- Link to relevant pages using descriptive anchor text
- Aim for 3–5 internal links per article
- Ensure your most important pages receive the most internal links
- Fix broken internal links immediately

### What to avoid:
- Generic anchor text ("click here", "read more")
- Linking to the same page multiple times in one article
- Orphan pages (pages with no internal links pointing to them)

## 9. Schema Markup

Schema markup (structured data) helps Google understand your content and can unlock rich results in search.

### Most valuable schema types for blogs:
- **Article / BlogPosting** — for blog posts
- **FAQPage** — for FAQ sections (can show expandable answers in results)
- **HowTo** — for step-by-step guides
- **BreadcrumbList** — for site navigation

Use JSON-LD format and test with Google's Rich Results Test.

## 10. Page Experience Signals

Beyond content, Google considers:
- **Core Web Vitals** (LCP, INP, CLS)
- **Mobile-friendliness** — test with Google's Mobile-Friendly Test
- **HTTPS** — non-secure pages are flagged in Chrome
- **No intrusive interstitials** — pop-ups that block content on mobile

## The On-Page SEO Audit Process

Run this audit on every important page quarterly:

1. Check title tag and meta description in Search Console (CTR data)
2. Review heading structure
3. Verify keyword placement
4. Check internal links (add any missing ones)
5. Test page speed in PageSpeed Insights
6. Validate schema markup
7. Review content for accuracy and freshness

On-page SEO is never "done" — it's an ongoing process of refinement based on performance data.
